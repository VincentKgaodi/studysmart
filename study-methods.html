Master the Art of Studying

Discover smart, proven study techniques tailored to your success

Pomodoro Technique

The Pomodoro Technique is a time management method that uses a timer to break work into intervals, traditionally 25 minutes in length, separated by short breaks. This technique helps improve focus and productivity.

Active Recall

Active recall is a study technique that involves actively stimulating your memory during the learning process. Instead of passively reviewing notes, you test yourself on the material, which enhances retention and understanding.

Spaced Repetition

Spaced repetition is a learning technique that incorporates increasing intervals of review to enhance long-term retention. By revisiting material at spaced intervals, you reinforce your memory and improve recall.

Mind Mapping

Mind mapping is a visual representation of information that helps organize and structure thoughts. It encourages creativity and aids in understanding complex concepts by connecting related ideas.

Study Groups

Joining a study group can enhance your learning experience. Collaborating with peers allows for diverse perspectives, shared resources, and accountability, making studying more effective and enjoyable.

Leitner system

Use flashcards with a box system. Cards you answer correctly move forward, while the ones you miss stay in the first box.

Blurting Technique

After studying, close your notes and “blurt” everything you remember onto paper. Then compare and fill in gaps.

Feynman Technique

earn a topic, then explain it in simple words like you're teaching a child. Gaps in understanding become obvious fast.

SQ3R Method

Survey, Question, Read, Recite, Review. A structured way to read textbooks or articles for full understanding.

Interleaving Practice

Instead of studying one subject in chunks, mix different topics in one session. It strengthens memory and flexible thinking.

Cornell Note-taking

Split your page into Notes, Cues, and Summary sections. Helps organize notes clearly and review faster.
